The Problem

The £68 billion telematics market has a critical intelligence gap. Current solutions collect massive amounts of data but fail to deliver the contextual insights needed to prevent accidents and reduce costs.

What's Wrong With Existing Solutions?

1. Generic Risk Scoring

Traditional telematics treats all "harsh braking" events equally, whether they occur on a dry motorway or a wet rural road at night. This one-size-fits-all approach creates false positives, frustrates drivers, and fails to identify true risk.

2. No Predictive Capability

Most solutions are reactive or near real-time at best. They tell you what happened but can't forecast which drivers are at highest risk of having incidents in the next 30/60/90 days.

3. Missing Behavioral Intelligence

Existing systems lack sophisticated driver profiling. They don't understand driver archetypes, adaptation patterns, or situational performance variations that are critical to targeted coaching.

4. Context Blindness

Weather conditions, road types, traffic density, time of day, and lighting are ignored. This eliminates the ability to understand why events occurred and whether they represent true risk.

The Business Impact

  • Ineffective Coaching: Fleet managers struggle to convert data into behavior change
  • Rising Costs: Insurance premiums increase despite telematics investment
  • Driver Turnover: Unfair scoring drives driver dissatisfaction and churn
  • Liability Risk: Inability to demonstrate proactive duty of care
  • Missed Savings: Can't target interventions at highest-risk drivers
