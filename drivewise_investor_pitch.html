The Problem: Data Without Intelligence

Current Solutions Fall Short

1. Context Blindness

Existing telematics flag "harsh braking" without understanding WHY. A panic stop on a wet motorway at night is treated the same as controlled braking on a dry urban road. Research shows context matters enormously, but commercial systems don't use it.

2. No Predictive Capability

Most solutions are reactive ("this happened") or near real-time at best. Insurers need 30/60/90-day incident probability forecasts to price risk accurately. This capability doesn't exist in commercial products.

3. Insurance Disconnect

Fleets invest in telematics expecting insurance savings, but premiums keep rising. Why? Because insurers can't translate generic telematics data into actuarially-sound risk reductions. The data format doesn't match underwriting needs.

4. Missing Behavioral Intelligence

No sophisticated driver profiling exists. Systems can't answer: "Is this driver cautious in urban areas but risky at night?" or "Does this driver adapt poorly to weather?"
