How Our Risk Engine Works

Our road risk engine combines historical crash data with environmental factors. By toggling the layers below, you can see how a "Safe" road in daylight can transform into a "High Risk" corridor in rain or darkness. We predict risk not just based on where accidents have happened, but where they are likely to happen right now.

Interactive Demo: Use the layer control (top right) to switch between 'Baseline', 'Rain + Daylight', and 'Rain + Darkness' scenarios.

The Science Behind the Score

1 Baseline Risk: Frequency × Severity

We calculate a weighted Severity Score for every road segment, giving more weight to fatal accidents than minor ones. Using advanced statistical modeling, we combine this historical data with traffic volume to identify true high-risk hotspots — smoothing out "freak accidents" while highlighting persistent dangers on specific road segments.

2 Dynamic Context Multipliers

Not all roads react to weather the same way. A straight highway might only become 1.2x riskier in rain, while a winding rural lane could become 3.5x riskier. Our engine learns and applies risk multipliers based on Weather and Light conditions, giving you a risk score that adapts to the changing environment.
